Hi there! I'm Sara

I'm a  

Growing up, I always knew I was passionate about design. I started by designing cheerleading uniforms and party invitations. By 2003, I started creating Blogspot and Xanga layouts for myself and anyone who wanted to customize their blog.

In 2006, I entered college and studied Political Science and put design on the back burner. I learned two important lessons which would later on prove to be helpful in my career:

  1. People will always have different opinions
  2. Empathy is important when crafting solutions for problems

After college, I moved to the United States and grew to become a strong user advocate in Customer Success & Experience for early-stage startups. I built teams, created customer journey maps and analyzed customer data to improve products. But the most exciting part of my role was participating in dogfooding sessions.

Dogfooding sessions made me realize the importance of design. We had to test the product to ensure that it was intuitive and made sense for our users. These sessions took me back to when I was a little girl taking vacations abroad with my family. No matter where I was, I could always tell where the bathroom or restaurants were because of the universal signs. This is how easy our product should end up being. This is how design, in general, should be.

This realization, coupled with my passion for design becoming unearthed, led me to Bloc.io's UX/UI Design Program. Using my work experience and lessons learned throughout my journey, I work on design projects that offer simple answers to complex user problems.

My goal as a designer is to craft stellar experiences that aren't only pleasing to the eye, but also effortless and inclusive. If we somehow share the same goal, then let’s connect!

Let's talk!

Let's chat about how we can create steallar user experiences, or anything and everything about UX over a cup of coffee.
